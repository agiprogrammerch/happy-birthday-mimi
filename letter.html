Dear Mimi,

Today the world is a little more beautiful because you exist in it. I know that sounds like something off a greeting card — but I mean it, genuinely, down to the last petal falling on this page.

I don't think you realize how much your warmth travels. Across time zones, across languages, across all the distances that the internet pretends don't exist — you carry light with you, and people around you end up carrying some of it too.

I'm really glad we became friends. I'm glad we talk about Japan and all the silly things that somehow matter. I'm glad that somewhere between conversations about nothing and everything, we made a promise to go see the sakura together someday.

On your birthday, I want you to know: you are seen, you are cherished, and you deserve every good thing this year is about to bring you.

May you get the kind of year that feels like a really good book — the kind you can't put down, full of surprising chapters and people who make the story worth reading. And somewhere in that story, I hope there's a page with cherry blossoms. Maybe even a real one.

Until then — happy birthday, Momo Akuta.
今日はあなたの特別な日です。

With warmth from Agra,

Aru-Chan 🌸
